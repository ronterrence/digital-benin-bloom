# Welcome to Digital Benin Bloom

## Project Structure Milestone

The exhibition is now organized into separate research universes:

- **Pitts Archive:** Source images and visual reconstruction from the 1900 Pitts publication.
- **Map:** Exploratory visual map of the Pitts image set.
- **Oxford Holdings:** Museum-side records from Oxford/Pitt Rivers material.
- **British Museum Holdings:** Metadata, images, acquisition data, provenance records, and current collection notes from the British Museum.
- **Met Museum Holdings:** Selected Metropolitan Museum of Art records related to Benin, Edo, Nigeria, and associated dispersal histories, treated as a working dataset pending manual review.
- **Context Images:** Original and restored historical context images supporting the narrative around 1897, dispersal, and reconstruction.
- **Research Layer:** Located / interpretive matches connecting historical figures from the Pitts publication to modern museum records.
- **Dispersal & Restitution Layer:** A developing layer for tracking institutional movement, current location, ownership-transfer status, restitution actions, and return agreements.

The project now separates the visual source archive from modern institutional records, while preserving a research bridge between them.

### Current Research Direction

The next major step is to strengthen the cross-reference layer connecting Pitts figures to modern museum records across Oxford, the British Museum, the Met Museum, and other institutional holdings.

The project will also begin documenting restitution and ownership-transfer cases, starting with records such as the MK&G Hamburg / MARKK Benin object record. This introduces a wider research question: not only where Benin objects are held, but how they moved, who currently owns them, what restitution actions have taken place, and what remains unresolved.

### Future Institutional Expansion

Future development may extend the holdings layer beyond Oxford, the British Museum, and the Met Museum to include collections in Vienna, Cambridge, Köln, Dresden, Hamburg, Leipzig, Stuttgart, the Netherlands, Scotland, Stockholm, and major museums in the United States, including Chicago, Boston, New York, Washington, and Philadelphia.

This may later become a dedicated **Dispersal Map / Institutional Holdings** section.

### Important Distinction

Artifact images and context images are treated as separate layers:

- **Artifact images** support object-level visual reconstruction and museum cross-referencing.
- **Context images** support historical atmosphere, narrative framing, and exhibition storytelling.
- **Institutional records** support provenance, acquisition, location, rights, and restitution research.
- **Restitution records** support tracking ownership-transfer status, return agreements, and unresolved institutional histories.
